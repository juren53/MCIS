## LIDO-Lightweight-Information-Describing-Objects-data-standard.md

Lightweight Information Describing Objects (LIDO) is an XML schema developed for sharing, harvesting, and aggregating cultural heritage metadata. Maintained by the CIDOC international committee for documentation, it allows museums and institutions to expose their collection objects—including art, natural history, and technology—to online portals and aggregation services. [1, 2, 3]  

Key Features of LIDO 

• Cross-Domain Utility: Designed to support a wide variety of material culture and natural heritage, rather than being limited to just art. 
• CIDOC CRM Compliance: It aligns with the CIDOC Conceptual Reference Model (CRM), organizing information around events (such as creation, finding, or use) rather than static categories. 
• Separation of Concerns: Distinctly groups display metadata (optimized for user presentation) and indexing metadata (optimized for search and retrieval). 
• Multilingual Support: Built to accommodate language variations, making it ideal for international aggregations like  Europeana . [2, 4]  

Where is it used? 

LIDO is heavily utilized for "data harvesting," where aggregation portals—such as national or thematic digital libraries—collect metadata directly from local museum databases, frequently via the OAI-PMH (Open Archives Initiative Protocol for Metadata Harvesting) framework. [3]
